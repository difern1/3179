An Insight Into Global Emissions

By Dinal Fernando 32511051

This visualisation explores the CO2 emissions of countries and regions in order to analyse the relative largest producers of CO2 emissions as well as connections between CO2 producers and measures such as quality of life and prosperity. In doing so, this visualisation allows for an understanding as to the forms of emissions produced, and the necessity of CO2 emissions for a nations prosperity which will potentially allow for a reduction in CO2 emissions globally.

The Global Per Capita Emissions

This choropleth map shows the Per capita emissions of countries. Use the dropdown menu to centre the map on various regions, and use the zoom to further interact with the map. As shown, Qatar has the highest per capita emissions with a population of 2.688 million. Further, there is a far lower concentration of higher producing countries in Africa. In the Americas, Canada and North America also feature relatively higher per capita emissions.
